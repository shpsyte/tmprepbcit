The Contemporary Art Gallery is an internationally acclaimed venue located in Vancouver, Canada and provides free admission to all.

The Contemporary Art Gallery (CAG) is an independent, non-profit public art gallery and one of Canada’s leading venues for contemporary art. Offering free access to everyone, we work with artists from Canada and around the world to produce exhibitions, off-site projects, residencies, learning, outreach, community engagement programs, events and publications. Each of these interdependent strands of programming varies in scale, duration and location to challenge how and where we might engage with contemporary visual art.

The Gallery

The Gallery displays temporary exhibitions across three rooms on the ground floor level. CAG shows work by artists from around the world and a variety of media is represented, including sound, film, mixed media, photography, painting, sculpture and installation. Free admission, donations welcome

Book Shop


The CAG Book Shop offers a range of publications, catalogues, artists’ limited editions and other materials for sale. As a non-profit charity, all revenue made from our shop goes straight back into supporting programming costs.

Resource Room


Located alongside our Book Shop is a visitor Resource Room, providing interpretive and contextual materials supporting our exhibitions, off-site projects and residencies. These resources include specially made artist files, books, texts and videos that open up multiple entry points into the work on display.

Off-site

As well as housing exhibitions within the gallery, CAG hosts additional off-site exhibitions at various locations year round. Our permanent off-site location is at Yaletown-Roundhouse Station on the corner of Mainland and Davie Streets. This public exhibition space facilitates the extension of our programming beyond the confines of the gallery and into the public realm, engaging with broader audiences and challenging conceptions of public art displays.
